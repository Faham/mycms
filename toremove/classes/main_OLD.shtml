New Classes in Games and Interactive Systems Design



Two new classes in Interactive Systems and Game Design will be offered in 2008-2009: CMPT 105 and CMPT 106. These classes will provide an introduction to interactive systems, design, and computing – no previous experience in Computer Science is required, and the classes can be taken by first-year students.

CMPT 105 and 106 can be used as Science credits for Arts students.

These classes are for students who want to focus on interaction and design, students who want a more gradual introduction to programming, and students who are not majoring in Computer Science but want some computing in their program.

How to Register: These are regular U of S classes that will start in September 2008 (CMPT 105) and January 2009 (CMPT 106). The official titles are:

  • CMPT 105.3: Introduction to Computing and Interactive Systems
  • CMPT 106.3: Design and Construction of Games and Interactive Systems

These courses have only recently been created, and so they do not appear in the offical U of S course calendar – but they are fully-approved regular classes. You can register through PAWS as you would for any other course. Please note that CMPT 105 is a prerequisite for CMPT 106.

For more information on the courses, visit the course pages for CMPT 105 and CMPT 106.
